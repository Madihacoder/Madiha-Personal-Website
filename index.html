    A Passion for Medical Imaging and AI: Advancing Healthcare through Innovation

    My research is dedicated to revolutionizing healthcare through the application of advanced medical imaging techniques and artificial intelligence. My primary focus lies in the development and application of innovative methods for early disease detection, precise diagnosis, and effective prognosis. I am particularly interested in leveraging MRI technology, coupled with cutting-edge AI, machine learning, and deep learning algorithms, to address critical challenges in oncology and neurology.

    Personal Journey and Motivation

    My journey in this field was deeply influenced by personal experiences. Witnessing my mother's battle with Osmotic Demyelination Syndrome, a rare neurological disorder,taking about three years of treatment of being physically impaired , unable to speak because of diaelated false cords despite treated with Plasmapheresis due to damaged Mylein Sheath, my Aunt battling stage 4 brain tumor despite chemo left bed ridden with just some months from the doctor to surive ignited my passion for finding innovative solutions to complex neurological and Cancerous challenges. Additionally, the widespread prevalence of cancer, especially in my region, has fueled my determination to contribute to early detection and improved outcomes.

    • Research Interests



    Building upon the strong foundation of medical imaging and AI, my research interests delve deeper into:



  • Advanced Medical Imaging Techniques:


    • Novel MRI acquisition sequences: accelerated MRI techniques and parallel imaging methods to reduce scan times and improve image quality.
    Quantitative MRI analysis:advanced methods for extracting quantitative biomarkers from MRI images, such as tissue relaxation times, perfusion metrics, and diffusion parameters.
    Multimodal imaging fusion: MRI with other imaging modalities (e.g., CT, PET) to provide more comprehensive information for diagnosis and treatment planning.

  • AI-Driven Medical Image Analysis:


    • Deep learning architectures: and optimizing deep neural networks (e.g., convolutional neural networks, recurrent neural networks) for tasks like image segmentation, object detection, and classification.
    Models:generative adversarial networks (GANs) for data augmentation, synthetic image generation, and anomaly detection.
    Explainable AI: interpretable models to understand the decision-making process of AI algorithms and improve their trustworthiness.

  • Clinical Applications:


    • Early disease detection:AI-powered tools for early detection of various diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and neurological disorders.
    Disease progression monitoring:disease progression using quantitative imaging biomarkers and AI-based models.
    Response assessment:the effectiveness of treatments based on imaging data and AI-driven analysis.
    Personalized medicine: AI to tailor treatment plans to individual patients based on their imaging characteristics and clinical factors.

  • Collaborations:


    • Partnerships:with clinicians to address real-world healthcare challenges and ensure the clinical relevance of research findings.
    Teams:with experts from various fields (e.g., radiology, oncology, cardiology, neurology) to tackle complex medical problems.
